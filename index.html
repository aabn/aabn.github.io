Anna Newman

Librarian & open knowledge advocate

About Me

I am a librarian who is passionate about open knowledge and public humanities. Currently I'm a Librarian for Digital Academic Strategies at Worcester Polytechnic Institute. I previously worked as the Open Access Specialist at Boston University and received my M.S. in Library and Information Science from Simmons College in 2016.

Open Knowledge

In my career as a librarian, I have advocated for open access to information, educated others about open access practices, and coordinated programming to promote the use of open educational resources. Read more about my work in open knowledge.

Public Humanities

I am also active in a number of public humanities projects. I am the web developer for the Audio Shelfie Project, a community storytelling project, and the Sojourner Truth Memorial Committee, a nonprofit organization that supports social justice efforts. I also started my own digital project to explore the lives of my great-great aunts during the Progressive Era and the intersections between their lives and my own. Read more about my work in public humanities.

Research Interests

My research interests include:

  • open access advocacy
  • gender and equity in scholarly communications
  • digital project management and sustainability
