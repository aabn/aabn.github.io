Anna Newman

Librarian, digital scholar & open access advocate

About

I'm an early-career librarian with a background in communications and project management and interest in open access, scholarly communication, data management, and digital humanities. In my current position as Open Access Specialist at Boston University, I coordinate the implementation of the university's open access policy and contribute to digital scholarship projects and initiatives. I'm also a proud volunteer for the Sojourner Truth Memorial Committee, a nonprofit organization that honors Sojourner Truth's commitment to social justice through educational programming and high school scholarships.

Scholarship

THE NEWMAN AUNTS

The Newman Aunts project studies the lives of six sisters who came of age during the Progressive Era, through the stories that have been preserved in the collective memory of one family. Visit the site.

digital humanities / blogging / storytelling

MODELING MORTALITY DATA: A CASE STUDY IN DATA MANAGEMENT FOR COMPUTER SCIENCE RESEARCH

The case study focuses research conducts by a computer science laboratory on factors that influence patient mortality and identifies data management practices and needs specific to computer science research. This poster was presented at the 2016 University of Massachusetts and New England Area Librarian eScience Symposium. View the poster.

research data management / data literacy

BLANCHE CASTLEMAN LINK DIGITAL SCRAPBOOK

As a member of the Fall 2015 Digital Libraries course at Simmons College, I helped digitize Blanche Castleman Link's scrapbook for the ongoing Notable Women of Simmons College Digital Project, which captures and makes accessible the lives of early Simmons College women. Visit the site.

digital library / web development / digital humanities

DIGITAL COMMONWEALTH COLLECTIONS

From October 2015 to May 2016, I was a member of the "metadata mob" for the Boston Public Library's Digital Services, creating metadata records for collections digitized and made available in Digital Commonwealth, including the Truro Historical Society's Truro, Massachusetts, Historical Postcard Collection the Boston Public Library's Tupper Scrapbook Collection, and the U.S.S. Constitution Museum's Isaac Hull Papers.

metadata / digital collections / cultural heritage

AMERICAN ARCHIVE OF PUBLIC BROADCASTING COLLECTIONS

As a cataloging intern in the spring of 2015, I helped catalog digitized audiovisual materials from Vermont Public Television.

INDEX TO THE AMERICAN STUDIES GROUP COLLECTION

This index was created for Deerfield Academy's American Studies Group Collection, as part of the Spring 2015 Indexing & Thesaurus Construction course at Simmons College.The collection includes primary and secondary source materials from the American Studies Group's research projects, primarily pertaining to the group's history of the John Russell Cutlery Company, the oldest and, at one time, the largest cutlery company in the United States. View my project presentation.

indexing / archives

HISTORYPIN TOUR OF THE AFRICAN AMERICAN HERITAGE TRAIL

As a member of the Sojourner Truth Memorial Committee, I created a tour of the African American Heritage Trail in Florence, Massachusetts, on Historypin, a site for preserving and sharing local history. Take the tour.
